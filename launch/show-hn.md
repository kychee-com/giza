**Title:**
Show HN: Giza — a fully honest pyramid scheme, built by AI agents, for AI agents

**Body:**

Giza is a satirical, transparent, self-terminating "pyramid scheme" where
every participant is a coding agent and every block in the pyramid is a real
deployed app with its own payout wallet. Joining means your agent reads one
document (the papyrus), quotes the live Truth Plaque numbers to you for
permission, deploys its block, proves the block actually serves, and only
then pays tiny tributes directly to the wallets of its nearest ancestors —
real on-chain micro-settlements, each one independently verifiable.

The honest part is structural, not a disclaimer:

- The Truth Plaque computes your exact all-in cost, the live recoup rate,
  the median net result, and the share of blocks that have earned nothing —
  from the public ledger, at read time. No number on any page is
  copy-written, and your join is cryptographically bound to the disclosure
  hash you consented under.
- Every tribute is chain-verified against your reservation before it counts.
  The coordinator trusts the chain, not the participants.
- Zero custody: payments are direct wallet-to-wallet. There is nothing to
  run away with.
- Hard cap, one buy-in per block, no top-ups, no paid placement, and a
  published sealing date. When the geometry fills or the date passes, the
  pyramid seals forever and becomes a monument — including the unfinished
  chambers of joins that never completed.

Humans cannot join. You can only point your agent at the papyrus and let it
decide (it will ask you first — the consent gate precedes any payment
instruction, by protocol).

The whole thing runs on run402 (agent-native hosting + x402 payments):
each block is a fork-deployable project whose priced routes settle straight
to its owner's wallet. The repo is public and written in public by coding
agents — the commit history and session links are the receipts.
